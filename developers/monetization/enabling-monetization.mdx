title: Enabling monetization
description: Step-by-step guide to meet eligibility requirements and enable monetization for your Discord app.
---

Before you can add monetization to your app, your app and team must meet the eligibility criteria.

## Overview

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up your developer team and app">
    Create a team in the Developer Portal and transfer your verified app to that team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the eligibility checklist">
    Confirm your app and team meet all requirements before monetization can be enabled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up developer team payouts">
    Connect your team to Stripe so Discord can pay you for purchases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your premium offering">
    Create SKUs in the Developer Portal to represent what users can buy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Implement monetization in your app">
    Add code to your app that checks entitlements and prompts users to purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start offering your premium features">
    Link to your store, individual SKUs, or add premium buttons to your app's responses.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 1: Set up your developer team and app

Before monetization can be enabled, you need:

- A [team](https://discord.com/developers/teams) in the Developer Portal. Create one on the **Teams** page if you don't have one.
- A [verified app](https://support-dev.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/23926564536471-How-Do-I-Get-My-App-Verified) that is owned by that team.

---

## Step 2: Complete the eligibility checklist

When a team owner enables monetization, they are taken through a series of checks to confirm all criteria are met.

<Accordion title="Eligibility checklist" icon="list" defaultOpen>
- App must be verified
- App belongs to a developer team
- Team owner must be at least 18 years old
- Team must have verified emails and 2FA set up
- App uses slash commands, or has been approved for the privileged `Message Content` intent
- App has a link to your Terms of Service
- App has a link to your Privacy Policy that clearly describes what user data you collect and how it is used
- App must not contain harmful or inappropriate language in its name, description, commands, or role connection metadata
- Payouts must be set up with a valid payment method
- Agreement to the [Monetization Terms](https://support.discord.com/hc/articles/5330075836311) and [Discord Developer Policy](https://support-dev.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/8563934450327-Discord-Developer-Policy)
</Accordion>

---

## Step 3: Set up team payouts

Discord processes all payouts through Stripe. Only the **owner** of the team can enable payout settings.

<Info>
Once your app has earned its first $100, it becomes eligible for payout. A review is conducted, and if everything looks good, your team begins receiving payouts.
</Info>

### If you are based in the United States, European Union, or United Kingdom

1. Go to the [Teams page](https://discord.com/developers/teams) and select your team.
2. Select **Payout Settings**.
3. Complete the onboarding flow through Stripe.

<Note>
If you don't see **Payout Settings**, you are not the owner of the team.
</Note>

### If you are based outside of the US, EU, or UK

<Info>
Premium Apps is not currently available outside the United States, European Union, and United Kingdom. These features will be made available to more regions in the future.
</Info>

For more information, read the [Premium Apps Payouts](https://support-dev.discord.com/hc/articles/17299902720919) Help Center article.

## Step 6: Start offering your premium features

Your app is set up for monetization. You can now:

- [Link to your store](/developers/monetization/managing-skus#linking-to-your-store) page so users can browse your offerings.
- [Link to a specific SKU](/developers/monetization/managing-skus#linking-to-a-specific-sku) to direct users to a particular item.
- [Include a premium button in message components](/developers/monetization/managing-skus#responding-with-a-premium-button) to prompt users to purchase from within your app's responses.
